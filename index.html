Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about cryptocurrency

  • What is cryptocurrency?

    Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security. It operates independently of a central bank and uses decentralized technology to let users make secure payments and store money without needing to use their name or go through a bank.

  • How do I get started with crypto?

    Start by choosing a trusted exchange, creating an account, and completing verification. Then you can buy your first crypto and store it in a wallet for security.

  • Is cryptocurrency safe?

    Crypto is secure when you use reputable platforms and protect your wallet keys. However, prices are volatile, so invest carefully.

  • What are gas fees?

    Gas fees are small payments users make to process transactions on a blockchain. They compensate network validators for their work.

  • What's the difference between a wallet and an exchange?

    An exchange is where you buy and trade crypto. A wallet is where you securely store it and control your private keys.

  • Can I reverse a crypto transaction?

    No. Crypto transactions are permanent once confirmed. Always double-check the address before sending.

  • What is staking?

    Staking is locking your crypto to support blockchain operations and earn rewards over time.

  • How are cryptocurrencies taxed?

    In most countries, crypto is taxed as capital gains or income, depending on how its used. Check your local tax regulations.
